## Why fundamentals beat hacks

Most hosts over-index on pricing hacks before their operation is stable. That creates short wins and long-term chaos. Start with a repeatable operating baseline: clear ownership, defined workflows, and weekly KPI reviews.

## The minimum operating dashboard

Track these metrics every week:

- Utilization rate by vehicle
- Revenue per available day
- Downtime by cause (maintenance, turnaround, scheduling)
- Booking lead time
- Guest response time

If one metric dips, make one operational change, then review impact next week.

## Build SOPs before scale

Create short, checklist-driven SOPs for your highest-risk workflows:

1. Vehicle intake and condition documentation
2. Turnover cleaning and photo verification
3. Maintenance triage and scheduling
4. Guest handoff communication

These SOPs reduce errors and make onboarding future team members easier.

## Decision cadence for operators

Run a 30-minute weekly ops review:

- What changed this week?
- Which vehicle underperformed and why?
- What is one fix to test this week?

This cadence helps you compound improvements over time.

## Common mistakes

- Expanding fleet size before process stability
- Tracking too many vanity metrics
- Letting ad hoc communication become default ops

## Final takeaway

Fleet growth is an operating discipline. Once your fundamentals are stable, automation and AI produce much stronger results.
