## Start with pricing bands, not random edits

Set three price zones for every vehicle:

- Floor price (protect occupancy and baseline margin)
- Target price (normal demand)
- Stretch price (high-demand windows)

This gives structure before optimization.

## Inputs that should move price

- Booking lead time
- Day-of-week demand pattern
- Local events and airport volume
- Competitor inventory compression

If you cannot explain the reason for a price move, do not make it.

## Weekly experiment framework

1. Pick one hypothesis.
2. Change one pricing variable.
3. Run for one full week.
4. Measure conversion, utilization, and rev/day.

Use this approach to avoid noisy conclusions.

## What to avoid

- Chasing competitor rates hourly
- Dropping below floor price too early
- Ignoring lead-time distribution in your market

## Final takeaway

Pricing wins come from systems and discipline. Dynamic pricing should be predictable internally, even when it feels adaptive externally.
